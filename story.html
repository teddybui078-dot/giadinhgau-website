Our story

How a too-small kitchen became home

Every family has an origin story. Ours involves a rice cooker, a pot of soup that refused to run out, and a table that kept getting longer.

← Back home
The long version

It started, as the best things do, with a kitchen too small for everyone and a pot of soup that somehow always stretched to feed one more.

Hùng and Mai arrived with two suitcases, a rice cooker, and a loose plan to figure the rest out as they went. The apartment was small enough that you could stir the soup, answer the door, and turn off the TV without taking a single step. Everyone said it was too cramped. Everyone came over anyway.

Over the years the table got longer, the laughs got louder, and the family grew its own gravity. Babies turned into Lego architects. A grandmother moved in with a lifetime of stories. A stray dog took one look at the place and decided it was home.

We are not a tidy family. We are a warm one — quick to tease, quicker to forgive, and always five minutes late because someone had to find their other shoe. This is the long version of how we got here, told the way Bà Ngoại tells it: out of order, with too many details, and absolutely worth it.

Milestones

How the years stacked up

The short list of moments that made us us — give or take a few hundred ordinary, perfect Tuesdays.

1998

Two suitcases and a rice cooker

Hùng and Mai land with barely more than a plan and a stubborn optimism. The rice cooker is, to this day, still going.

2001

The kitchen that started it all

First apartment, first too-small kitchen, first pot of soup that mysteriously fed everyone who showed up. A tradition is born.

2004

Enter Bảo

Papa Bear takes up barbecue. It is the world's slowest barbecue. Nobody minds, because the company is the point.

2008

Enter Linh

The sticker era begins. So does the running tally of oddly specific whale facts that nobody asked for and everybody now knows.

2012

Bà Ngoại moves in

Bedtime stories get longer, better, and occasionally never-ending. House rule established: you do not interrupt the story.

2016

Cookie adopts us

A stray dog wanders in, sits down, and refuses to leave. From this day forward, every snack is officially a shared snack.

2021

The cabin by Pine Lake

We find the little cabin two hours north. The wifi is terrible. We have never been happier to be unreachable.

2026

This little website

A place to keep the good stuff — the faces, the trips, the in-jokes that make no sense to anyone else. Welcome in.

The soup always stretches. You just have to set another place.
