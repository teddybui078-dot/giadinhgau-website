The cabin

A little place that holds everyone

It is not a big cabin. It has a porch that fits four if two of them are friendly, a kitchen that has hosted more arguments about board games than anywhere on earth, and a screen door that has never once closed quietly.

We found it in 2021 and have been coming back ever since. The lake is freezing in June and somehow colder in August. There is exactly one bar of signal, and you have to stand on the second porch step to get it, which everyone agrees is a feature, not a problem.

The itinerary

What we actually do up there

Ambitious plans are made on the drive up. None of them survive contact with the porch.

1

Brave the lake

Someone always swears the water "isn't that cold." Someone is always wrong, loudly, and immediately.

2

Lose at board games

The rules are house rules, the stakes are personal, and at least one alliance ends in tears every visit.

3

Eat far too much

The slowest barbecue in the known world, followed by the kind of nap that feels earned.

4

Do absolutely nothing

The best part. No signal, no schedule, just the screen door and the sound of the lake.

Getting there

Head north until the radio gives up, then a little further.

Take the highway out of the city for about an hour and a half. When you pass the gas station with the good donuts, stop — that part is not optional. Then it's another half hour of smaller and smaller roads until the pavement turns to gravel and the trees close in.

You'll know you're close when the phone goes quiet. The cabin is the one with the crooked porch light and somebody already waving from the steps.

That's the whole tour.

Thanks for wandering through. The door's always open — come back soon.
